#### What is Whistleblowing

Whistleblowing is the voluntary release of nonpublic information, as a moral protest, by a member or
former member of an organization outside the normal channels of communication to an appropriate
audience about illegal and/or immoral conduct in the organization or conduct in the organization
that is opposed in some significant way to the public interest.

- Criteria for it to qualify as Whistleblowing
    - Who can blow the whistle? An individual inside an organization.
    - What kind of information is involved? | Nonpublic information that reveals new facts.
    - Who is informed and how? External: An employee &quot;goes public&quot; to inform individuals
      or groups outside the organization.Internal: An employee informs others within the
      organization by going outside normal channels of communication. An employee follows
      established procedures to report wrongdoing through normal channels of communication.
    - Was the person compelled to act? The release of information is voluntary.
    - What is the motive? To stage a moral protest.

#### Justification of Whistleblowing

- The Loyal Agent Argument -- fiduciary relationship -- the main job of the agent is to act in the
  interests of the principal
    - Is this all there is to loyalty?

- Are Whistle-blowers disloyal?

    - According to the LAA it seems on first glance /she is

    - One is only expected to do what is reasonable not whatever one is asked to do; hence not
      illegal or immoral -- but this is debatable

    - Obligations of loyalty are limited to the needs of the relationship.

    - Employees are hired for limited purposes

#### The Meaning of Loyalty

- Acts of "disloyalty" are done out of loyalty to the organization (or profession)

    - Loyalty as commitment to the true interests of the organization and not certain individuals or
      groups of individuals in an organization

    - Who or what kinds of relations typically involve expectations of loyalty?

        - Involves betrayal and breaking relations of trust

#### Conditions of justified Whistleblowing

- The situation is of sufficient seriousness

- You've done your homework -- diligently acquired all the facts of the case

- Exhausted all alternative channels

- Internal or external?

- What actions does one have credible responsibility to blow the whistle over?

- Reasonable chance of success

#### Developing a Policy

- Effectively communicated statement

- Clear definition of procedure for reporting

- Well trained personnel to receive and investigate reports

- Commitment to take appropriate action

- Guarantee against retaliation (!!!!)
